Gym

Keaney gym is a very important building on campus. This building is located in between the Ryan center, and the Ice hockey rink, and it is also a short walk from Eddy and Weldin hall. This building houses a numerous amount of sports teams, as well as facilities for those teams.
Keaney gym is used by both the Mens and Womens volleyball teams, and contains locker rooms for baseball, soccer, tennis, and track and field. It also has athletic offices for football, soccer, baseball, golf, and tennis.

History

        The gym is named after Frank Keaney, who coached basketball from 1920, to 1940. He led them to an overall record of 401-124, or a winning percentage of .764 through 28 seasons. Keaney also coached the football team from 1920 to 1940, and was the second-winningest football coach in URI history. Keaney sadly passed away in 1967 at the age of 81. The Keaney gym opened in 1953, and has a capacity of 3385 people. Even now, given the size of the building, it serves as a perfect hub for anything athletic or sports related.

        Unbeknownst to many, Keaney gym used to hold concerts, with notable artists such as The Beach Boys, Red Hot Chili Peppers, and Bob Dylan. From 1967 to 1969 the gym used to hold the first round games for the NCAA tournament. Overall, Keaney has made a lasting impression on the University of Rhode Island, and I believe that this facility is a great way to honor his accomplishments.
